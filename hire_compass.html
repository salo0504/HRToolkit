HIRE Compass helps employers understand work authorization, sponsorship needs, compliance steps, and next actions when hiring international, refugee, and immigrant employees. Built for HR teams and hiring managers, the platform translates complex immigration information into practical, plain-language guidance.

Hiring foreign-born workers is not only an inclusion strategy. It is a workforce strategy.

Across the United States, international, refugee, and immigrant workers help employers reduce turnover, fill hard-to-staff roles, expand recruitment pipelines, bring language and cultural skills, and contribute to innovation and growth.

01
Build a more stable workforce

Turnover is expensive. A study by the Fiscal Policy Institute and the Tent Partnership for Refugees found that 73% of employers reported higher retention among refugee employees than among other workers. Employers also said refugee hiring helped widen their candidate pool and strengthen referrals.

02
Fill roles employers are already struggling to staff

Immigrants make up 18.8% of U.S. healthcare workers, including more than 3.1 million healthcare workers based on 2024 American Community Survey data. In construction, immigrants made up 26.3% of the U.S. construction workforce in 2024.

03
Bring innovation, entrepreneurship, and new ideas

In 2025, 231 of the Fortune 500 — 46.2% — were founded by immigrants or their children, generating $8.6 trillion in revenue. 25% of U.S. billion-dollar startups had an international-student founder, creating an average of 860 jobs each.

04
Serve more customers and communities

U.S. job postings seeking bilingual workers more than doubled in five years — from roughly 240,000 in 2010 to about 630,000 in 2015. Companies with immigrant employees are also more likely to export to those employees' countries of origin.

Real stories from employers who got it right.

These are real examples of what happens when employers invest in foreign-born talent.

Retention turnaround
From 300% turnover to 5–15%

Denver-based L&R Pallet struggled with annual turnover of about 300%. After building a refugee hiring pathway and investing in workplace supports, turnover fell to between 5% and 15%.

Filling critical vacancies
Three years of vacancies filled in weeks

At Salem Hospital, newly arrived work-authorized migrants helped fill six environmental services openings after the department had been short-staffed for about three years.

Innovation pipeline
From international student to Zoom founder

Eric Yuan first came to the U.S. through an employment pathway, became one of WebEx's early engineers, and later founded Zoom. 25% of billion-dollar startups had an international-student founder.

Market reach
Bilingual hire captures a market segment

At a GameStop store in New York, hiring an English-Spanish bilingual employee meant Spanish-speaking customers who would have gone to competing stores now came to that location.

For most foreign-born candidates, hiring is no different from any other hire.

For most candidates, it's like hiring any US worker. For some, it's different but manageable. For a few, you'll work with an immigration attorney — and we'll show you exactly when and how.

Verify and move on
US citizens, green card holders, refugees, asylees

Same I-9 you do every day. Nothing extra to track.

Standard hire + calendar reminder
TPS, OPT, STEM OPT, H-4 EAD, parolees

Standard I-9 plus expiration tracking and reverification.

Bring in an immigration attorney
H-1B, L-1, O-1, TN

Employer files a petition. HR coordinates; counsel handles legal.
